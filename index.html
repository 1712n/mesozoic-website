Academic and Engineering Partnership

Universities and industry participants are coming together to tackle growing security problems.

We are trying to shift the balance from the development of speaker recognition systems, largely applicable to government surveillance systems, towards the speaker verification technologies, mostly used in civilian authentication systems.

While larger companies continue to release black box software with little to no regard for scientific backing and long-term viability, our teams are getting their research peer-reviewed and implemented in the open-source code.

Unique Dataset

Presented at INTERSPEECH 2018 conference

Our team members have compiled a unique dataset, which included 229 speakers spanning across an average of 18.9 years per speaker. This dataset has allowed us to glimpse into the change of extracted voice features over time and enabled the subsequent research into longitudinal speaker verification modeling.
